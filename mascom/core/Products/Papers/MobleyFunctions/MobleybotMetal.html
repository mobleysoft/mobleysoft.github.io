Mobleysoft Fiferbotics Platform:

Towards a True Automaton and Computational Metallurgy

0. Abstract

This paper introduces the **Mobleysoft Fiferbotics Platform**, a framework integrating **mechanics, computation, and fractal material science** into a unified automaton. We develop **Fiferbot Mark 1**, a hybrid **clockwork-digital humanoid** robot optimized for **energy efficiency, motion stability, and computational material design**. Key contributions include novel mathematical models solving **three-lever systems, three-pulley systems, three-body systems, and three-pendulum systems**.

1. Introduction

Traditional humanoid robots rely heavily on digital actuation and discrete control systems. However, leveraging **continuous mechanics** inspired by classical automata can improve **motion efficiency** and **energy conservation**. This paper proposes a **computronium-based robotics approach**, where **metal itself** serves as both the **mechanical substrate** and **computational medium**.

2. Mathematical Foundations

2.1 Lagrangian Dynamics for Energy Optimization

The Lagrangian function \( L \) is defined as:

\[ L = T - V \]

where \( T \) represents **kinetic energy** and \( V \) represents **potential energy**. For a system of two moving masses:

\[ L = \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2 + \frac{1}{2} m_2 v_2^2 - (m_1 g h_1 + m_2 g h_2) \]

2.2 Three-Lever System for Force Transmission

A **lever system** optimizes force distribution, modeled as:

\[ F_{\text{output}} = \frac{d}{l} F_{\text{input}} \]

where \( d \) is the **distance from the pivot** and \( l \) is the **lever arm length**.

2.3 Three-Pulley System for Mechanical Advantage

We define the efficiency coefficient \( \eta \) as:

\[ F_{\text{output}} = \eta \cdot \frac{F_{\text{input}}}{N} \]

where \( N \) is the number of pulleys, and \( \eta \) accounts for frictional losses.
